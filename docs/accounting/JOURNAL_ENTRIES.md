# Journal Entries (Double-Entry Posting Core) — QAYD Accounting Engine
Version: 1.0
Status: Design Specification
Module: Accounting
Submodule: Journal Entries
---

# Purpose

The Journal Entries submodule is the single transactional core of the QAYD Accounting Engine. Every
financial fact that will ever appear in a General Ledger, a Trial Balance, or a Financial Statement in
QAYD passes through this submodule in exactly one form: a balanced journal entry composed of a
`journal_entries` header and two or more `journal_lines`. No other table in the platform is permitted to
represent a posted financial fact. Sales, Purchasing, Banking, Inventory, Payroll, and Tax do not maintain
their own parallel ledgers; they raise domain events that this submodule consumes to produce journal
entries, and they read back GL balances by querying posted `journal_lines` (or the `ledger_entries`
projection) rather than computing their own numbers.

This document specifies, at implementation-ready depth, how a journal entry is created (manually by an
accountant, automatically by another module, or proposed by an AI agent), how it moves through its
lifecycle from draft to posted, how the posting engine enforces the double-entry invariant
`SUM(debits) = SUM(credits)`, how approval and period-lock controls gate posting, and how the data model,
API surface, permissions, validation, AI responsibilities, notifications, reporting, audit trail,
performance, background processing, and security around journal entries are built in Laravel 12 (PHP 8.4)
on PostgreSQL, exposed to a Next.js 15 frontend, and assisted by a FastAPI Python AI layer that never
writes to the database directly.

The submodule exists to guarantee three properties that the rest of the platform — and every downstream
consumer, from the CFO's dashboard to an external auditor's sample pull — depends on absolutely:

1. **Correctness.** Every posted journal entry is balanced, dated inside an open fiscal period, references
   only active postable accounts, and cannot silently disappear or be silently altered.
2. **Immutability.** Once a journal entry is posted, its header and lines are frozen. Every correction is a
   new, fully traceable financial event (a reversing or adjusting entry), never an edit.
3. **Explainability.** Every entry — whether typed by a human, generated by the Sales module on invoice
   creation, or proposed by the AI layer — carries enough metadata (source, reference, memo, confidence,
   reasoning, approval chain, and full history) that any authorized user or auditor can reconstruct, without
   asking anyone, exactly why a number appears where it appears in the books.

# Vision

QAYD's long-term vision for this submodule is a General Ledger that closes itself. Concretely:

- **Near-zero-touch bookkeeping for routine transactions.** Invoices, bills, payments, standard payroll
  runs, straight-line depreciation, and opening balances should never require a human to open a journal
  entry screen. The Posting Engine consumes domain events and produces balanced entries automatically,
  with the AI layer providing a second pass of anomaly and fraud screening before anything posts.
- **Real-time, always-reconcilable books.** Because `ledger_entries`, the Trial Balance, and every
  Financial Statement are derived views over posted `journal_lines`, and because posting is transactional
  and immediate (no nightly batch), a company's GL should be correct to the second, at any hour, without a
  month-end scramble.
- **AI as a tireless junior accountant, never an unsupervised signer.** AI agents draft entries, explain
  them in plain language (English and Arabic), attach a calibrated confidence score, and flag anything that
  smells like a duplicate or a fraud pattern — but sensitive or high-value postings still require a human
  in the approval chain, by policy, always.
- **An audit trail strong enough to hand to a regulator unmodified.** Every state transition, every field
  change before posting, and every reversal is recorded permanently. A journal entry's full life story
  (who drafted it, what the AI suggested, who approved it, when it posted, whether and why it was reversed)
  is retrievable in one API call, in production, years later.
- **A posting engine that scales from a five-employee Kuwait retailer to a multinational holding company**
  without a rewrite: partitioned tables, projected read models, and asynchronous background jobs are part
  of the design from day one, not bolted on after the first performance incident.

# Double Entry Accounting

Double-entry accounting is the structural rule that every recorded financial event affects at least two
accounts, in equal and offsetting amounts, such that the accounting equation always holds:

```
Assets = Liabilities + Equity
```

Every journal entry in QAYD is a set of `journal_lines`, each line either a **debit** or a **credit**
against exactly one account, such that:

```
SUM(line.debit) = SUM(line.credit)     -- enforced at posting, to the smallest currency unit
```

This is not a UI convention; it is enforced as a hard invariant by the Posting Engine (see
"Posting Engine") and backed by a database CHECK constraint on the header's cached totals as a defense-in-
depth measure. An entry that does not balance can exist as a `draft`, but it can never reach `posted`.

## Normal balances by account type

Each account inherits a **normal balance** from its `account_type`. A "normal balance" is the side
(debit or credit) on which an increase to that account is recorded. This table is canonical across QAYD
and every module that produces automatic journal entries must respect it:

| Account Type | Increases with | Decreases with | Normal Balance | Appears on |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Asset | Debit | Credit | Debit | Balance Sheet |
| Expense | Debit | Credit | Debit | Income Statement |
| Liability | Credit | Debit | Credit | Balance Sheet |
| Equity | Credit | Debit | Credit | Balance Sheet |
| Revenue | Credit | Debit | Credit | Income Statement |
| Contra-Asset (e.g. Accumulated Depreciation) | Credit | Debit | Credit | Balance Sheet (netted against Asset) |
| Contra-Revenue (e.g. Sales Returns) | Debit | Credit | Debit | Income Statement (netted against Revenue) |

## Worked example

A KWD 1,050.000 sales invoice (KWD 1,000.000 goods + KWD 50.000 output VAT at 5%) issued by the Sales
module produces this journal entry automatically (see "Automatic Entries → Invoices" for the full
template):

| Line | Account | Debit (KWD) | Credit (KWD) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 1120 · Accounts Receivable — Control | 1,050.000 | — |
| 2 | 4100 · Sales Revenue | — | 1,000.000 |
| 3 | 2310 · Output VAT Payable | — | 50.000 |
| **Total** | | **1,050.000** | **1,050.000** |

The entry increases an Asset (Accounts Receivable, debit-normal, increased by a debit — correct), increases
Revenue (credit-normal, increased by a credit — correct), and increases a Liability (VAT Payable,
credit-normal, increased by a credit — correct). The accounting equation stays balanced: Assets increase by
1,050.000; Liabilities increase by 50.000; Equity (via retained earnings, once the period closes) will
increase by 1,000.000.

## Debit/credit sign convention in the schema

QAYD never stores a signed single "amount" column on `journal_lines`. It stores two non-negative columns,
`debit` and `credit`, with a CHECK constraint that exactly one of them is non-zero per line (see
"Database Design"). This is deliberate: signed-amount schemas are a well-known source of sign-flip bugs in
ERP systems and make SUM-based balance checks fragile. Two non-negative columns make
`SUM(debit) = SUM(credit)` a trivial, unambiguous, injection-proof aggregate query.

# Accounting Theory

The Journal Entries submodule is the enforcement point for several accounting-theory principles that QAYD
adopts platform-wide. This section documents each principle and exactly how the system enforces or
supports it — because a specification that merely names GAAP/IFRS principles without wiring them into
concrete system behavior is not implementation-ready.

**Accrual basis of accounting.** QAYD posts revenue and expense when they are *earned* or *incurred*, not
when cash changes hands. Concretely: a sales invoice posts revenue and an AR debit on the invoice date; the
cash receipt against it later posts a *separate* entry that debits Bank/Cash and credits AR — it never
re-touches revenue. This is why "Payments" and "Receipts" have their own automatic-entry templates distinct
from "Invoices" and "Bills" (see "Automatic Entries").

**Matching principle.** Expenses are recognized in the same period as the revenue they helped generate.
QAYD supports this at the journal-line level through the `cost_center_id`, `project_id`, and
`department_id` dimensions, which let the Reports submodule match costs to the revenue-generating unit that
incurred them, and through accrual/deferral adjusting entries (a `Manual Entries` use case) at period end
— e.g. accruing an expense incurred in period N but not yet billed.

**Revenue recognition.** Revenue is recognized when control of goods/services transfers to the customer
(IFRS 15 / ASC 606 alignment), which in QAYD's Sales module is the invoice/delivery event, not the order or
the payment. Multi-period service contracts recognized over time are handled via deferred-revenue liability
accounts and a recurring `journal_entry_templates` schedule that releases revenue period by period.

**Materiality.** QAYD does not hard-code a materiality threshold into posting logic (materiality is a
judgment call, not a validation rule), but it exposes `custom_fields` and reporting filters so a Finance
Manager can flag and review entries above a company-configured materiality threshold, and the Approval
Workflow's amount-based routing (see "Approval Workflow") is effectively a materiality-driven control.

**Consistency.** Once a company selects an accounting policy (e.g. depreciation method, inventory
valuation method), the relevant automatic-entry template is fixed for that company for the fiscal year;
changing it mid-year requires an explicit "accounting policy change" adjusting entry with a mandatory memo,
which the Audit Trail records distinctly from ordinary corrections.

**Going concern & full disclosure.** These are financial-statement-level concerns handled by the Reports
submodule, but the Journal Entries submodule supports them by never deleting historical financial data
(soft delete only) and by keeping every entry's supporting `attachments` permanently retrievable.

**Historical cost vs. fair value / currency translation theory.** Transactions are recorded at the
exchange rate in effect on the transaction date (`journal_lines.exchange_rate`), producing a
**functional-currency (base-currency) amount** that never changes after posting (historical cost). Foreign-
currency-denominated balances that remain open (e.g. an unpaid foreign-currency invoice) are revalued at
period end using a *separate, distinct* Currency Revaluation journal entry (see "Automatic Entries →
Currency Revaluation") that adjusts the base-currency carrying value without touching the original
transaction-currency amount — this is the standard "monetary item retranslation" treatment under IAS 21.

**Control accounts and sub-ledgers.** Accounts Receivable and Accounts Payable are *control accounts* in
the Chart of Accounts (`accounts` table, `is_control_account = true`). Individual customer/vendor balances
are *never* posted as separate GL accounts; they are tracked via `journal_lines.customer_id` /
`journal_lines.vendor_id` against the single control account, and the Customers/Vendors sub-ledgers must
always reconcile 1:1 to the control account balance. The Posting Engine enforces that any line carrying a
`customer_id` or `vendor_id` must target an account flagged as a control account of the matching sub-ledger
type — this is a validation rule, not a suggestion (see "Validation Rules").

**Double-entry as fraud deterrent.** Because every value movement requires an equal offsetting entry
somewhere else in the books, and because posted entries are immutable, double-entry bookkeeping is itself a
control: a party trying to hide a misappropriation must either fabricate a fictitious offsetting account
(discoverable by Trial Balance review) or manipulate two independent entries consistently (much harder,
and exactly the pattern the AI Fraud Detection agent screens for — see "AI Responsibilities").

# Journal Entry Lifecycle

A journal entry moves through a strict finite state machine. `status` on `journal_entries` is the single
source of truth for where an entry sits in its life; no other flag or timestamp may contradict it.

## States

| State | Meaning | Mutable? | Who can be in this state |
|---|---|---|---|
| `draft` | Created but not yet submitted. Header/lines fully editable. | Yes — full edit | Manual entries; automatic entries pending completeness (rare); AI-suggested entries awaiting human review |
| `pending_approval` | Submitted; waiting on one or more approval levels. | No line/amount edits; submitter may withdraw back to `draft` | Any entry whose amount/type requires approval per policy |
| `approved` | All required approval levels have signed off; queued for posting. | No | Entries about to be posted by the Posting Engine (usually the same request cycle) |
| `rejected` | An approver declined it. | Returns to `draft` for correction (see transition table) | Any entry that failed approval |
| `posted` | Committed to the General Ledger. `journal_lines` are now immutable and drive `ledger_entries`. | **No mutation whatsoever** | Every entry that has taken financial effect |
| `reversed` | A posted entry that has been fully offset by a system-generated reversing entry. | No (the reversing entry itself is a new, separate `posted` entry) | Corrected posted entries |
| `voided` | A posted entry cancelled via same-period reversal where the reversing entry exactly negates it and both are hidden from "active" balance views by convention (still fully retained). | No | Entries that were posted in error and caught within the same open period |
| `archived` | Posted (or reversed/voided) entry moved out of active working views for performance/UI decluttering. Purely a visibility flag; carries full financial weight forever. | No financial mutation; `archived` flag itself is togglable by CFO/Finance Manager | Old fiscal-year entries |

Note on scope: the task's required states are Draft, Pending, Approved, Posted, Reversed, Archived. QAYD's
implementation adds `rejected` and `voided` as necessary intermediate/sibling states because a pure
6-state machine cannot represent "approval declined" or "same-period cancellation" without conflating them
with `draft` or `reversed`; both are documented above and included in the transition table below so the
model remains internally consistent and fully specified.

## State transition table

| From | To | Trigger / Endpoint | Guard conditions |
|---|---|---|---|
| *(none)* | `draft` | `POST /journal-entries` (manual) or Posting Engine intake (automatic/AI) | `company_id`, at least 2 lines, valid accounts |
| `draft` | `pending_approval` | `POST /journal-entries/{id}/submit` | Entry balances; period open; all required fields present |
| `draft` | `posted` | `POST /journal-entries/{id}/post` | Only if entry type/amount requires **no** approval (see "Approval Workflow" thresholds) |
| `pending_approval` | `approved` | `POST /journal-entries/{id}/approve` (final level) | Actor holds `accounting.journal.approve`; not the entry's own creator unless single-person company exception is configured |
| `pending_approval` | `pending_approval` | `POST /journal-entries/{id}/approve` (non-final level) | Advances `approval_level`; status unchanged |
| `pending_approval` | `rejected` | `POST /journal-entries/{id}/reject` | Any assigned approver at the current level |
| `rejected` | `draft` | `POST /journal-entries/{id}/resubmit-as-draft` (implicit on edit) | Creator or Finance Manager edits and the record is reopened |
| `approved` | `posted` | Posting Engine (synchronous, immediately after final approval, or via `POST /journal-entries/{id}/post`) | Period still open; accounts still active; balance re-verified |
| `posted` | `reversed` | `POST /journal-entries/{id}/reverse` | Creates and posts a new balanced reversing entry; original flips to `reversed` and links via `reversal_entry_id` |
| `posted` | `voided` | `POST /journal-entries/{id}/reverse` with `same_period=true` and same-day reversal | Cosmetic distinction of `reversed`; original fiscal period unaffected |
| `posted` \| `reversed` \| `voided` | `archived` | `POST /journal-entries/{id}/archive` | No open dependency (e.g. not the target of a pending reconciliation) |
| `archived` | `posted` \| `reversed` \| `voided` | `POST /journal-entries/{id}/unarchive` | Restores visibility only; no financial change ever occurred |
| `draft` | *(deleted — soft)* | `DELETE /journal-entries/{id}` | Only `draft` or `rejected` states are hard-eligible for soft delete |

   create ─────► │   draft   │◄───────────────────┐
                 └─────┬─────┘                     │ reject
                       │ submit                    │

                ┌──────────────────┐         ┌──────┴──────┐

Every transition above writes one row to `journal_entry_history` (full JSONB snapshot) and one row to the
foundation `audit_logs` table (who/when/old value/new value/reason/IP/device), per the platform-wide
mutation-audit rule. See "Audit Trail."

# Manual Entries

Manual entries are journal entries typed directly by a human accountant through the Next.js journal-entry
form, rather than raised automatically by another module. They are the mechanism for everything the system
cannot infer on its own.

## Primary use cases

| Use case | Typical lines | Typical creator role |
|---|---|---|
| Adjusting entry (accrual) | Dr Expense / Cr Accrued Liability | Senior Accountant, Accountant |
| Adjusting entry (deferral) | Dr Prepaid Asset / Cr Expense, or Dr Deferred Revenue / Cr Revenue | Senior Accountant |
| Reclassification | Dr Account A / Cr Account B (net zero across statement) | Accountant |
| Write-off (bad debt, obsolete inventory adjustment authorized outside Inventory module flow) | Dr Bad Debt Expense / Cr AR (customer-tagged) | Finance Manager, CFO |
| Owner/equity transaction (capital injection, drawings, dividend declaration) | Dr/Cr Equity account, Dr/Cr Cash/Bank | CFO, Owner |
| Correction of a prior manual entry (via reversal + new correct entry) | Full reversal of original + freshly drafted correct entry | Senior Accountant, Finance Manager |
| Intercompany allocation (single company scope; true intercompany elimination is a Future Improvement) | Dr Expense (receiving cost center) / Cr Expense (allocating cost center), same account, different `cost_center_id` | Finance Manager |

## Manual-entry creation workflow

1. Accountant opens **New Journal Entry** in the Next.js app; `entry_type` defaults to `manual`.
2. Header fields are entered (journal date, currency, memo, reference — see "Journal Header").
3. Lines are added one at a time (minimum two); each line requires an account and either a debit or a
   credit amount, and may carry `cost_center_id`, `project_id`, `department_id`, `tax_code_id`.
4. The client computes a running balance indicator (`total_debit − total_credit`) so the accountant sees
   the imbalance in real time before attempting to submit; the server re-validates independently and does
   not trust the client's arithmetic.
5. Attachments (e.g. a signed accrual worksheet, a board resolution for a dividend) are attached via the
   polymorphic `attachments` table (`attachable_type = 'journal_entry'`).
6. Accountant clicks **Submit**. If the entry type/amount requires approval, status becomes
   `pending_approval`; otherwise it posts immediately (subject to the Posting Engine's own re-validation).

## Templates and recurring manual entries

Recurring manual entries (monthly rent accrual, monthly depreciation top-up not otherwise automated,
quarterly loan-interest accrual) are defined once as a `journal_entry_templates` row with a
`recurrence_pattern` and a `line_template` JSONB payload describing each line as either a fixed amount or a
formula (e.g. `"= balance_of(account:2210) * 0.01"` for a 1%-of-balance provision). A scheduled background
job (`GenerateRecurringJournalEntries`, see "Background Jobs") materializes a new `draft` (or, if
`auto_post = true` and the template is pre-approved by policy, directly `posted`) journal entry from the
template on each due date, always crediting the originating template on `journal_entries.source_type =
'journal_entry_template'` / `source_id` for full traceability.

## Manual-entry-specific validation

- A manual entry's `journal_date` may be backdated only within the current or a still-open prior period
  (see "Fiscal Period Rules"); backdating into a locked period is rejected outright and never silently
  reassigned to the current date.
- A manual entry touching a control account (AR/AP) **must** carry a `customer_id`/`vendor_id` on that
  line; the UI enforces this before submission and the server enforces it again (see "Validation Rules").
- A manual entry may not be the vehicle for a transaction type that has a dedicated automatic-entry
  template (e.g. an accountant cannot "manually" post a sales invoice's revenue recognition bypassing the
  Sales module) — `entry_type = 'manual'` combined with a `source_type` matching a reserved automatic
  source (e.g. `'invoice'`) is rejected by the Service layer.

# Automatic Entries

Automatic entries are journal entries the Posting Engine generates on behalf of another module in response
to a **domain event**, never by direct cross-module database writes (per platform rule). Each source module
publishes an event; a dedicated Laravel Listener (e.g. `App\Listeners\Accounting\PostInvoiceJournalEntry`)
translates the event's payload into a `journal_entries` + `journal_lines` payload using the templates below,
then calls the same internal `JournalEntryService::create()` / `::post()` that manual entries and the API
use — there is exactly one code path into the ledger, regardless of origin.

Every automatic entry sets `journal_entries.entry_type`, `source_module`, `source_type`, and `source_id` so
it is always traceable back to the exact invoice/bill/payroll run/etc. that caused it, and it is *never*
directly editable by a human (see "Locking Rules" — automatic entries can only be corrected via reversal).

## Invoices (Sales)

Triggered by `invoice.created` (or `invoice.confirmed`, depending on company posting-point policy) from the
Sales module (`invoices`, `invoice_items`).

| Line | Account | Debit | Credit |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Accounts Receivable — Control (tagged `customer_id`) | Invoice total (incl. tax) | — |
| 2 | Sales Revenue (per product category / revenue account mapping) | — | Invoice subtotal (excl. tax) |
| 3 | Output VAT/Tax Payable | — | Tax amount |
| 4 (if applicable) | Discount Allowed (contra-revenue) | Discount amount | — |

Cost-of-goods-sold recognition for inventory-tracked items posts as a **second, linked** automatic entry
(see "Inventory" below) at the same moment, keeping revenue recognition and inventory relief as two
distinct, independently traceable entries rather than one overloaded posting.

## Bills (Purchasing)

Triggered by `bill.created` / `bill.approved` from Purchasing (`bills`, `bill_items`).

| Line | Account | Debit | Credit |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Expense or Inventory Asset (per bill line's account mapping) | Bill subtotal | — |
| 2 | Input VAT/Tax Receivable | Tax amount | — |
| 3 | Accounts Payable — Control (tagged `vendor_id`) | — | Bill total (incl. tax) |

## Payments (Banking / AP disbursement)

Triggered by `vendor_payment.completed` from Banking/Purchasing (`vendor_payments`, `bank_transactions`).

| Line | Account | Debit | Credit |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Accounts Payable — Control (tagged `vendor_id`) | Payment amount | — |
| 2 | Bank Account (specific `bank_accounts` GL mapping) | — | Payment amount |
| 3 (if early-payment discount taken) | Bank Account | — | Discount amount |
| 3b | Purchase Discount (income/contra-expense) | — | Discount amount (offsetting split of line 3, or combined per company policy) |

Customer **receipts** are the AR mirror image: Dr Bank, Cr Accounts Receivable — Control (tagged
`customer_id`), with an optional Dr Sales Discount / Cr AR split for early-payment discounts granted.

## Inventory

Triggered by `stock_movement.created` (issue on sale, receipt on purchase, adjustment, transfer valuation
delta) from Inventory (`stock_movements`, `inventory_valuations`).

| Event | Debit | Credit |
|---|---|---|
| Goods issued on sale (COGS relief, at weighted-average or FIFO cost per company policy) | Cost of Goods Sold | Inventory Asset |
| Goods received on purchase (already captured by the Bill template above when the bill lines are inventory-mapped; a **separate** entry is only needed for goods received before billing) | Inventory Asset (Goods Received Not Invoiced) | GRNI Accrual (liability) |
| Positive stock adjustment (count found more than book) | Inventory Asset | Inventory Adjustment Gain (other income) |
| Negative stock adjustment / shrinkage / write-off | Inventory Shrinkage Expense | Inventory Asset |
| Inter-warehouse transfer with valuation change (e.g. landed-cost allocation) | Inventory Asset (destination warehouse) | Inventory Asset (source warehouse) |

## Payroll

Triggered by `payroll_run.completed` from Payroll (`payroll_runs`, `payroll_items`, `salary_components`).

| Line | Account | Debit | Credit |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Salaries & Wages Expense (gross, per department/cost-center split from `payroll_items`) | Gross payroll | — |
| 2 | Employee GOSI/Social-Insurance Payable (employee portion withheld) | — | Employee-portion withholding |
| 3 | Employee Income-Tax/Other Withholding Payable (where applicable by jurisdiction) | — | Withholding amount |
| 4 | Employer GOSI/Social-Insurance Expense (employer portion) | Employer contribution expense | — |
| 5 | Employer GOSI/Social-Insurance Payable | — | Employer contribution payable |
| 6 | Net Salaries Payable (or directly Bank, if paid same run) | — | Net pay |

A follow-on **Payments** entry (Dr Net Salaries Payable / Cr Bank) posts when disbursement actually occurs,
consistent with the accrual-then-settle pattern used for invoices/bills.

## Depreciation

Triggered by the monthly/period-end `RunAssetDepreciation` background job reading `assets` (owned by the
Assets module) depreciation schedules.

| Line | Account | Debit | Credit |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Depreciation Expense (per asset category / cost-center) | Period depreciation | — |
| 2 | Accumulated Depreciation (contra-asset, same asset category) | — | Period depreciation |

## Loans

Triggered by `loan_disbursed`, `loan_repayment_scheduled`/`loan_repayment_made`, and period-end
`loan_interest_accrual` events.

| Event | Debit | Credit |
|---|---|---|
| Loan disbursed to company | Bank Account | Loan Payable (long-term or current, per term) |
| Scheduled repayment — principal portion | Loan Payable | Bank Account |
| Scheduled repayment — interest portion | Interest Expense | Bank Account (or Interest Payable if accrued first) |
| Period-end interest accrual (not yet paid) | Interest Expense | Interest Payable |
| Loan issued **by** the company (note receivable) | Notes Receivable | Bank Account |

## Assets

Triggered by `asset.acquired`, `asset.disposed`, `asset.revalued` from the Assets module in addition to the
Depreciation template above.

| Event | Debit | Credit |
|---|---|---|
| Asset acquired by cash purchase | Fixed Asset (category account) | Bank Account / Accounts Payable |
| Asset disposed — sold above net book value | Bank/AR (sale proceeds); Accumulated Depreciation (to date) | Fixed Asset (gross cost); Gain on Disposal (revenue) |
| Asset disposed — sold below net book value | Bank/AR (proceeds); Accumulated Depreciation; Loss on Disposal (expense) | Fixed Asset (gross cost) |
| Asset revaluation (upward, if company policy permits revaluation model) | Fixed Asset | Revaluation Surplus (equity, OCI) |

## Taxes

Triggered by `tax_transaction.created` (per-transaction, already largely captured inline in the Invoices/
Bills templates above) and by `tax_return.filed`/`tax_return.paid` (period settlement) from the Tax module.

| Event | Debit | Credit |
|---|---|---|
| VAT return filed — net payable to authority | Output VAT Payable (clearing the accumulated balance) | Input VAT Receivable (clearing) + Tax Payable to Authority (net) |
| VAT return filed — net refundable from authority | Tax Refund Receivable (net) + Output VAT Payable (clearing) | Input VAT Receivable (clearing) |
| Payment of tax liability to authority | Tax Payable to Authority | Bank Account |
| Withholding tax remitted on behalf of a vendor | Withholding Tax Payable | Bank Account |

## Currency Revaluation

Triggered by the period-end `RevalueForeignCurrencyBalances` background job, which retranslates every open
monetary balance (unsettled foreign-currency AR/AP, foreign-currency bank accounts) at the period-end spot
rate.

| Event | Debit | Credit |
|---|---|---|
| Foreign-currency asset/receivable balance increases in base-currency value (rate moved favorably) | Asset/AR account (base-currency adjustment only; transaction-currency amount untouched) | Unrealized FX Gain (income) |
| Foreign-currency asset/receivable balance decreases in base-currency value | Unrealized FX Loss (expense) | Asset/AR account |
| Foreign-currency liability/payable balance increases in base-currency value | Unrealized FX Loss (expense) | Liability/AP account |
| Foreign-currency liability/payable balance decreases in base-currency value | Liability/AP account | Unrealized FX Gain (income) |

Revaluation entries are always tagged `entry_type = 'revaluation'` and are the *only* automatic-entry type
permitted to touch the `base_debit`/`base_credit` columns without touching `debit`/`credit` (transaction
currency), since by definition only the base-currency carrying value is being restated.

## Year Closing

Triggered manually by a CFO/Owner action (`POST /api/v1/accounting/fiscal-years/{id}/close`, owned by the
Fiscal Periods module) once every period in the fiscal year is `locked`. The Journal Entries submodule
executes the resulting **closing entry**:

| Line | Account | Debit | Credit |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1..n | Every Revenue account (closing its year-end balance to zero) | Full credit balance (as a debit to zero it) | — |
| n+1..m | Every Expense account (closing its year-end balance to zero) | — | Full debit balance (as a credit to zero it) |
| Final | Retained Earnings (equity) | (if net loss) | (if net income) |

This single closing entry is system-generated, always `entry_type = 'year_closing'`, always requires CFO or
Owner approval regardless of amount, and is the entry that establishes next year's Opening Balances.

## Opening Balances

Two scenarios: (a) a brand-new company onboarding onto QAYD with pre-existing balances, and (b) the
automatic roll-forward at fiscal-year start.

| Event | Debit | Credit |
|---|---|---|
| New-company onboarding: every Asset/Expense account's opening balance | Asset/Expense account | Opening Balance Equity (suspense) |
| New-company onboarding: every Liability/Equity/Revenue account's opening balance | Opening Balance Equity (suspense) | Liability/Equity/Revenue account |
| Fiscal-year roll-forward: every Balance-Sheet account's closing balance | Carries forward automatically as the new year's opening balance — no new entry needed; only Income Statement accounts are closed (see Year Closing) | — |

`entry_type = 'opening_balance'` entries are exempt from the normal "must reference an existing fiscal
period with prior activity" rule (there is no prior period) but are otherwise validated identically, and
the temporary Opening Balance Equity suspense account must net to zero once all opening balances are
entered — the UI blocks fiscal-year activation until it does.

## AI Generated Entries

Triggered when a FastAPI AI agent (General Accountant, Document AI/OCR Agent, Forecast Agent) proposes a
journal entry — e.g. from an OCR'd vendor bill without a matching Purchasing record, from a bank-statement
line with no matching `bank_transactions` reconciliation candidate, or from a detected recurring pattern
the AI believes warrants an accrual.

| Field | Behavior |
|---|---|
| `entry_type` | `ai_generated` |
| `status` on creation | Always `draft` — **never** `pending_approval` or `posted` directly from the AI layer |
| `ai_confidence` | Required, `0.0000`–`1.0000`; populated by the FastAPI layer's model output |
| `ai_conversation_id` | Links to the `ai_conversations`/`ai_messages` thread that produced the reasoning, for full explainability |
| Debit/credit template | None fixed — the AI proposes accounts and amounts per line, each carrying its own `journal_lines.ai_confidence` and, where the AI second-guessed itself, `ai_suggested_account_id` distinct from the line's final `account_id` |
| Posting path | AI submits the draft via the same `JournalEntryService::create()` used by manual entries, through the Laravel API (never direct DB write); a human must review and submit/approve/post exactly as with any manual draft, **unless** the company has explicitly enabled auto-post for a narrow, pre-approved entry class (e.g. bank-fee entries under a fixed small-amount threshold) — see "AI Responsibilities → Automatic Posting" |

# Posting Engine

The Posting Engine is the only code path in the entire platform authorized to write a row into
`journal_lines` with `journal_entries.status = 'posted'`. It is implemented as
`App\Services\Accounting\JournalEntryPostingService` and is invoked identically whether the trigger is a
human clicking **Post**, a Sales-module Listener reacting to `invoice.created`, a scheduled background job
running `RunAssetDepreciation`, or the AI layer's draft being approved by a human. There is no bypass.

## Posting algorithm

```
BEGIN TRANSACTION (PostgreSQL, isolation level: READ COMMITTED with explicit row locks)

1. SELECT the journal_entries row FOR UPDATE (row lock prevents a concurrent post/edit race).
2. Re-validate status: must be 'approved', or 'draft' AND entry_type/amount is within the
   no-approval-required threshold. Any other status → 409 Conflict, abort.
3. Re-validate balance: SELECT SUM(debit), SUM(credit) FROM journal_lines WHERE journal_entry_id = :id
   AND deleted_at IS NULL. If SUM(debit) <> SUM(credit) → 422 Unprocessable Entity, abort.
   (This re-derives from the lines table itself — it never trusts the cached header totals.)
4. Resolve fiscal_period_id from journal_date if not already set; SELECT the fiscal_periods row
   FOR UPDATE; if status <> 'open' → 409 Conflict ("period is locked/closed"), abort.
5. For every line: verify account_id resolves to an active, postable account in company_id;
   verify cost_center_id/project_id/department_id/branch_id (if set) are active and belong to
   company_id; verify customer_id/vendor_id presence rule for control accounts.
6. Compute base_debit/base_credit per line from debit/credit × exchange_rate, rounded per the
   account's currency decimal precision; recompute and re-persist header cached totals.
7. Assign journal_number from journal_number_sequences (SELECT ... FOR UPDATE on the sequence row,
   increment, release) if not already assigned (manual drafts get a provisional number at creation;
   automatic/AI entries get their real number only at posting to avoid gaps from abandoned drafts —
   see "Edge Cases").
8. UPDATE journal_entries SET status = 'posted', posted_by, posted_at = now(), locked = true.
9. INSERT into ledger_entries (the GL read-model projection) one row per journal_line — synchronous,
   in the same transaction, so Trial Balance queries are never stale even by a second.
10. INSERT one journal_entry_history snapshot row (change_type = 'posted').
11. INSERT one audit_logs row.
12. COMMIT.
13. AFTER COMMIT (outside the transaction, via Laravel's afterCommit queued listener):
    - Dispatch domain event journal.posted (company_id, journal_entry_id, entry_type, source_type,
      source_id, total, currency_code) for Reverb real-time push, webhooks, and cross-module
      subscribers (e.g. AR/AP sub-ledger balance caches).
    - Dispatch notifications per "Notifications".
```

If any step from 2–7 fails validation, the transaction rolls back entirely; the entry remains in its
pre-post status (`approved` or `draft`) and the API returns a structured 422/409 error identifying exactly
which line or which rule failed — never a generic failure.

## Balancing tolerance

QAYD enforces **zero tolerance** on the balance check: `SUM(debit) − SUM(credit)` must equal `0.0000`
exactly, in the entry's own currency and independently in base currency. Rounding differences that arise
from multi-currency lines are resolved by requiring the last line of a multi-currency entry to be a
plug-rounding line against a dedicated `Rounding Gain/Loss` account when the discrepancy is below
`0.0100` in base currency (configurable per company); anything larger is a hard validation failure, not an
auto-plug (see "Edge Cases").

## Idempotency for automatic entries

Every Listener that posts an automatic entry from a domain event includes an idempotency key
(`source_module + source_type + source_id + event_name`) checked against `journal_entries.source_type` +
`source_id` before creating a new entry; if a matching non-deleted entry already exists for that exact
source/event, the Listener is a no-op. This prevents duplicate postings from event redelivery (e.g. a
queue retry after a transient failure) — see also "AI Responsibilities → Duplicate Detection" for the
AI-layer's independent second check on manually/AI-drafted entries.

# Approval Workflow

Not every journal entry requires approval before posting, but every entry above a company-configured
threshold, and every entry of a sensitive type, always does — this is a platform-wide non-negotiable rule
(sensitive operations always require a human approval chain).

## Approval routing

| Condition | Approval requirement |
|---|---|
| `entry_type = 'manual'` and base-currency total < company's Tier-1 threshold (e.g. KWD 500) | No approval required; `accounting.journal.post` holder may post directly from `draft` |
| `entry_type = 'manual'` and total between Tier-1 and Tier-2 threshold (e.g. KWD 500–5,000) | 1 approval level: Senior Accountant or above |
| `entry_type = 'manual'` and total ≥ Tier-2 threshold (e.g. ≥ KWD 5,000) | 2 approval levels: Senior Accountant/Finance Manager, then CFO or Owner |
| `entry_type = 'ai_generated'` (any amount) | Minimum 1 approval level, always — AI drafts are never auto-postable except a narrowly whitelisted auto-post class (see "AI Responsibilities") |
| `entry_type = 'year_closing'` | Always requires CFO or Owner, regardless of amount |
| `entry_type IN ('invoice','bill','payment','receipt','inventory','payroll','depreciation','tax')` (automatic, system-originated) | No approval required by default (the *source document* — e.g. the bill itself, the payroll run — already went through its own module's approval); company policy may opt specific types into a review queue without blocking posting (post-hoc review, not pre-post gating) |
| `entry_type = 'revaluation'` | 1 approval level: Finance Manager or CFO |
| Any manual reversal of a posted entry | 1 approval level: Finance Manager or CFO, regardless of the original entry's amount |

Thresholds and the automatic-type review-queue opt-in are stored per-company in a `company_id`-scoped
settings record (owned by the platform Settings module, referenced here, not redefined).

## `journal_entry_approvals` mechanics

Submitting an entry that requires approval creates one `journal_entry_approvals` row per required level,
`status = 'pending'`, in ascending `approval_level` order. Approving level *n* sets that row's `status`,
`approver_id`, `decided_at`; if a further level exists, the entry stays `pending_approval` and a
notification routes to the next level's eligible approvers; if level *n* was final, the entry transitions
to `approved` and the Posting Engine is invoked immediately in the same request (synchronous auto-post on
final approval, unless the company disables this and requires an explicit separate **Post** action).

Rejecting at any level immediately sets the entry to `rejected`, closes all pending approval rows for that
entry as `status = 'skipped'`, and notifies the original creator with the `decision_reason`.

## Delegation

An approver may delegate a specific pending approval to another eligible user (`delegated_to`); the
delegate acts with full authority for that approval only, and both the delegator and delegate are recorded
permanently on the row and in `audit_logs`.

## Segregation of duties

The Posting Engine enforces that the `created_by` user of an entry may never also be its sole approver at
any level, unless the company has exactly one qualifying user in that role (a documented, audited
exception for very small companies) — enforced as a Validation Rule, not a UI-only nudge.

# Locking Rules

1. **Posted immutability.** No field on a `posted`, `reversed`, `voided`, or `archived` journal entry or
   any of its lines may ever change via `UPDATE`. The `JournalEntryService` exposes no update path for
   these statuses; the API layer returns `403 Forbidden` for any edit attempt; and a PostgreSQL trigger
   (`trg_journal_lines_no_update_when_posted`, see "Database Design") is a second, database-level line of
   defense independent of application-layer bugs.
2. **Draft/rejected mutability with optimistic concurrency.** `draft` and `rejected` entries are fully
   editable, but every write must supply the `version` it last read; a mismatch returns `409 Conflict`
   ("this entry was modified by someone else — reload and retry") rather than silently overwriting a
   concurrent edit.
3. **Control-account posting restriction.** Only automatic entries and manual entries explicitly tagged
   with a `customer_id`/`vendor_id` may post to a control account; a plain manual entry attempting to debit
   or credit the AR/AP control account without a sub-ledger tag is rejected — this keeps the sub-ledger
   always reconcilable to the control account.
4. **Account-level lock.** An account flagged `accounts.status = 'inactive'` cannot receive new postings
   (existing historical postings remain untouched and fully reportable); attempting to post to it fails
   validation with a clear "account is inactive" error, distinct from "account does not exist."
5. **Period lock (see "Fiscal Period Rules" for full detail).** No entry may post into, nor may a draft be
   dated into, a `locked` or `closed` fiscal period.
6. **Sequence lock.** `journal_number_sequences.next_number` increments under a row-level `FOR UPDATE` lock
   so two concurrent posts in the same company/fiscal-year/entry-type never receive the same number.
7. **Reversal-of-reversal lock.** An entry that is itself a system-generated reversal
   (`journal_entries.is_reversal = true`) cannot itself be reversed directly by a user action; correcting a
   bad reversal requires a fresh manual adjusting entry with an explicit memo, preventing an unbounded chain
   of reversals-of-reversals from obscuring the audit trail.

# Fiscal Period Rules

Fiscal structure is owned by `fiscal_years` and `fiscal_periods` (Fiscal Periods module), but the Journal
Entries submodule is the primary consumer and enforcer of period state at posting time.

## Period states

| State | Meaning | Can post into it? | Can draft-date into it? |
|---|---|---|---|
| `future` | Period not yet reached; exists for forward planning (e.g. recurring templates) | No | Only for pre-dated recurring templates explicitly allowed to generate ahead |
| `open` | Current, actively postable period | Yes | Yes |
| `soft_close` | Company has begun period-end procedures; only Finance Manager/CFO may post; standard accountants blocked | Restricted (elevated role only) | Restricted |
| `locked` | Period-end closing checklist complete; no further postings without explicit reopening | No | No |
| `closed` | Fiscal year closing entry has run and this period is permanently sealed for the year | No, ever, without a formal reopening + re-audit procedure | No |

## Period-end closing checklist (enforced sequence, owned collaboratively with Fiscal Periods/Reports)

1. All automatic-entry sources for the period reconciled (bank reconciliation complete, all invoices/bills
   for the period posted, payroll run posted).
2. Currency Revaluation entry posted for the period, if the company holds foreign-currency monetary items.
3. Depreciation entry posted for the period.
4. Accrual/deferral manual adjusting entries posted and approved.
5. Trial Balance for the period reviewed and signed off by Finance Manager/CFO (recorded as a
   `report_runs` row referencing the review).
6. Period transitions `open → soft_close → locked`.
7. At fiscal-year end only, after every period in the year is `locked`: the Year Closing entry runs (see
   "Automatic Entries → Year Closing") and every period in that fiscal year transitions to `closed`.

## Reopening a locked/closed period

Reopening is never a casual action. It requires `accounting.period.reopen` (Owner/CFO only per the
Permissions section), a mandatory reason recorded in `audit_logs`, and automatically re-notifies the
External Auditor role if one is configured for the company, since reopening a previously-locked period is
itself an auditable event regardless of whether any new entry is ultimately posted into it.

## Backdating rule

A `journal_date` may be set to any date within the entry's resolved fiscal period **at the time of
posting** — not at the time of draft creation. This matters because a draft can sit for days: if the
period it was dated into becomes `locked` before the draft is posted, posting fails with a period-lock
error and the accountant must either re-date the entry into the current open period (with a memo explaining
the redate) or request the elevated reopening procedure above. The system never silently moves a draft's
date forward on the user's behalf.

# Database Design

The Journal Entries submodule owns six tables. All follow the platform's standard-column rule
(`id`, `company_id`, `branch_id`, `created_by`, `updated_by`, `created_at`, `updated_at`, `deleted_at`)
except where explicitly noted (`journal_entry_history` is append-only and has no `updated_at`/`deleted_at`
by design — history rows are never edited or deleted). `accounts`, `account_types`, `cost_centers`,
`projects`, `departments`, `branches`, `fiscal_years`, `fiscal_periods`, `customers`, `vendors`,
`tax_codes`, `tax_rates`, `ai_conversations`, `companies`, and `users` are **referenced, not owned** by this
document — they are defined by the Chart of Accounts, Dimensions, Fiscal Periods, Customers, Vendors, Tax,
and AI Platform module specifications respectively.

companies 1───* journal_entries *───1 fiscal_periods

journal_entries 1───* journal_entry_approvals
journal_entries 1───* journal_entry_history

-- Defense-in-depth: block any UPDATE to a line whose parent entry is posted/reversed/voided/archived.
CREATE OR REPLACE FUNCTION fn_block_update_when_posted() RETURNS TRIGGER AS $$
DECLARE v_status journal_entry_status;
BEGIN
  SELECT status INTO v_status FROM journal_entries WHERE id = OLD.journal_entry_id;
  IF v_status IN ('posted','reversed','voided','archived') THEN
    RAISE EXCEPTION 'journal_lines: cannot modify a line of a % entry (id=%)', v_status, OLD.journal_entry_id;
  END IF;
  RETURN NEW;
END;
$$ LANGUAGE plpgsql;

CREATE TRIGGER trg_journal_lines_no_update_when_posted
  BEFORE UPDATE OR DELETE ON journal_lines
  FOR EACH ROW EXECUTE FUNCTION fn_block_update_when_posted();

`journal_entry_history` is intentionally distinct from the generic `audit_logs` table: `audit_logs` stores
one row per *field-level* change (old value/new value pairs) for platform-wide compliance queries, while
`journal_entry_history` stores a full JSONB snapshot of the entry-plus-lines at each lifecycle transition,
optimized for a single fast "show me exactly what this entry looked like at version N" API call without
reconstructing state from many `audit_logs` rows.

# Permissions

All permission keys follow the platform's `<area>.<entity>.<action>` convention and are default-deny: a
role with no explicit grant cannot perform the action, full stop.

| `accounting.journal.read` | View journal entries and their lines/history | Owner, CEO, CFO, Finance Manager, Senior Accountant, Accountant, Auditor, Read Only, External Auditor |
| `accounting.journal.create` | Create a new draft (manual or via AI draft review) | Owner, CEO, CFO, Finance Manager, Senior Accountant, Accountant |
| `accounting.journal.update` | Edit a draft/rejected entry the user is permitted to touch | Owner, CFO, Finance Manager, Senior Accountant, Accountant (own drafts only, unless Finance Manager/CFO) |
| `accounting.journal.submit` | Submit a draft for approval | Finance Manager, Senior Accountant, Accountant |
| `accounting.journal.approve` | Approve or reject at an assigned approval level | CFO, Finance Manager, Senior Accountant (per threshold) |
| `accounting.journal.post` | Directly post an approval-exempt entry | Finance Manager, CFO, Senior Accountant (below Tier-1 threshold only) |
| `accounting.journal.reverse` | Create a reversing entry against a posted entry | CFO, Finance Manager |
| `accounting.journal.delete` | Soft-delete a draft/rejected entry | Senior Accountant, Finance Manager, CFO |
| `accounting.journal.archive` | Archive/unarchive posted entries (visibility only) | Finance Manager, CFO |

configuration; this is enforced in the Laravel Policy layer, not merely hidden in the UI.

# Validation Rules

Validation runs at three layers, from earliest/cheapest to latest/most authoritative: (1) client-side
in the Next.js form for instant feedback, (2) the Laravel `FormRequest` for request-shape and per-field
rules, (3) the `JournalEntryService`/`JournalEntryPostingService` for business invariants that require a
database read (balance, account status, period status) — only layer 3 is trusted; layers 1–2 exist purely
for UX responsiveness.

| Rule | Enforced at |
|---|---|
| Entry has at least 2 non-deleted lines | FormRequest + Service |
| `SUM(debit) = SUM(credit)` in transaction currency and in base currency | Service (re-derived from DB, at submit and again at post) |
| Every line has exactly one of `debit`/`credit` > 0, the other exactly `0.0000` | FormRequest + DB CHECK |
| `journal_date` resolves to an existing fiscal period for `company_id` | Service |
| Resolved fiscal period `status = 'open'` (or `soft_close` with elevated role) | Service, re-checked at post time even if it passed at submit time |
| Every `account_id` belongs to `company_id`, is `status = 'active'`, `is_postable = true` | Service |
| A line targeting a control account (AR/AP) carries the matching `customer_id`/`vendor_id` | Service |
| A line targeting an account flagged `requires_cost_center = true` carries a `cost_center_id` | Service |
| `currency_code` on a line, if ≠ header currency, requires an explicit `exchange_rate` and company policy allowing per-line currency override | Service |
| `exchange_rate` has an available rate source for `currency_code` as of `journal_date` (from the FX rate table owned by the Currency module) when not manually overridden | Service |
| `entry_type ≠ 'manual'` requires non-null `source_module`, `source_type`, `source_id` | FormRequest + Service |
| `ai_generated = true` requires non-null `ai_confidence` and `ai_conversation_id`, and every line requires `ai_confidence` | FormRequest + Service |
| Creator of an entry may not be its sole/final approver, except the documented single-user exception | Service |
| `journal_number` is immutable post-assignment and unique per `(company_id, journal_number)` | DB UNIQUE constraint |
| A `draft`/`rejected` edit must supply the current `version`; mismatch → `409` | Service (optimistic lock) |
| Reversal date must fall in an open period ≥ the original entry's `journal_date` | Service |
| Attachment `attachable_type = 'journal_entry'` rows must belong to the same `company_id` as the entry | Service |
| Recurring-template-generated draft's `line_template` amounts/formulas must resolve to a fully balanced set before draft creation | Background job pre-check |

# AI Responsibilities

The FastAPI AI layer participates in this submodule through several distinct agents, all bound by the
platform rule that AI **never writes to the database directly** — every AI action is a call into the
Laravel API, subject to the same permission checks, validation rules, and approval routing as a human
action, and every AI output carries confidence, reasoning, and supporting evidence.

| Agent | Role in Journal Entries | Inputs | Outputs | Autonomy |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **General Accountant** | Drafts `ai_generated` entries from unmatched bank lines, OCR'd bills without a Purchasing record, or detected recurring patterns | Bank statement lines, OCR'd documents, historical posting patterns | A draft journal entry (via the standard create API), `ai_confidence`, natural-language reasoning stored in `ai_messages` | Suggest-only; drafts always land in `draft` and require human submit/approve |
| **Document AI / OCR Agent** | Extracts vendor/amount/tax/date from a scanned bill or receipt attached to a draft entry, proposing line-level account codings | Attachment image/PDF | Proposed `account_id` per line (`ai_suggested_account_id` if a human later overrides), `tax_amount` extraction, per-line `ai_confidence` | Suggest-only |
| **Fraud Detection** | Screens entries at submission time for known fraud patterns: round-trip entries between related accounts, entries just under an approval threshold, unusual posting-time-of-day, first-time account/cost-center combinations at high value | All entries at `submit` | A risk score attached to the entry's `custom_fields`; on high risk, forces a mandatory Finance Manager/CFO review step regardless of the normal threshold routing | Requires-approval escalation only — it can never block posting outright without a human review, but it can *add* an approval level |
| **Duplicate Detection** | Compares a new draft (manual, automatic, or AI-generated) against recent entries with matching amount, date proximity, account set, and `reference`/source similarity | New drafts at creation and at submit | A warning flag + list of candidate duplicate `journal_entry_id`s surfaced in the UI before submit | Suggest-only; never auto-rejects |
| **Suggested Entries** | Proactively proposes recurring/adjusting entries the AI believes are missing at period end (e.g. "no rent accrual posted for a month that historically always has one") | Historical posting cadence per account/cost-center | A draft entry pre-filled from the historical pattern, flagged `ai_generated` | Suggest-only |
| **Explanation** | On request (`GET /journal-entries/{id}/ai-explanation`), generates a plain-language, bilingual (EN/AR) narrative of why an entry exists and why its accounts/amounts were chosen | The entry, its lines, its `ai_conversation_id` thread if present | Human-readable explanation text, not stored as a new DB row (generated on demand from `ai_messages` + entry data) | Read-only, no side effects |
| **Confidence Score** | Every AI-authored line and entry carries a calibrated `0.0000`–`1.0000` score reflecting the model's certainty in the proposed account/amount/dimension coding | Model output at generation time | `ai_confidence` field | N/A — a data attribute, not an action |
| **Learning** | When a human overrides an AI-suggested account (`ai_suggested_account_id ≠ account_id` at submit/approve time), that correction is logged and periodically exported to the FastAPI training pipeline to recalibrate future suggestions per company | `journal_lines` override diffs | Improved future `ai_confidence` calibration and account-suggestion accuracy; no live/online retraining inside the transactional path | Passive/offline learning only — never affects an already-posted entry |

## Confidence-gated auto-post whitelist

A narrow exception exists: a company may explicitly whitelist a specific, low-risk `ai_generated` entry
pattern (e.g. "known recurring bank fee under KWD 10, matched against a specific bank account and a
specific expense account, confidence ≥ 0.98") for direct auto-post without human approval. This whitelist is
company-configured, requires CFO sign-off to enable, is itself an auditable `audit_logs` event, is capped
per-entry and per-day in total value, and is reviewed on a recurring cadence — it does not weaken the
platform-wide rule that sensitive operations require human approval; it narrowly defines a class of
entries pre-approved by policy rather than per-instance.

# Notifications

Journal-entry lifecycle transitions raise notifications through the foundation `notifications` table and
Laravel Reverb for real-time in-app delivery; high-priority events (approval overdue, period reopened) also
fan out to email/SMS per the user's notification preferences.

| Event | Recipients | Channel |
|---|---|---|
| `journal.submitted_for_approval` | Users holding the required approval role at the current level | In-app (Reverb) + email digest |
| `journal.approval_overdue` (pending > 48h, configurable) | Original approver(s) + their manager | In-app + email |
| `journal.rejected` | Entry creator | In-app + email |
| `journal.posted` | Entry creator; Finance Manager/CFO for entries above the materiality threshold | In-app |
| `journal.reversed` | Entry creator, original approver(s), Finance Manager | In-app + email |

# Reports

Every report below is a **derived view** over posted `journal_lines` (via the `ledger_entries` projection)
— none maintains independent primary data:

- **General Ledger** — chronological posted line detail per account, with running balance.
- **Trial Balance** — period-end debit/credit balance per account, must always net to zero.
- **Journal Register** — every posted (and, optionally, draft/pending) entry for a date range, for audit
  sampling.
- **Journal Entry Audit Report** — entries filtered by `created_by`, `ai_generated`, or approval chain, for
  internal control review.
- **AI-Generated Entries Report** — every `ai_generated` entry with its confidence score, human override
  rate, and approval outcome, used to monitor AI reliability over time.
- **Pending Approvals Report** — all `pending_approval` entries older than a configurable age, routed to
  Finance Manager/CFO as an exceptions list.
- **Reversal/Adjustment Report** — every `reversed`/`voided` entry paired with its reversing entry and
  reason, for control-effectiveness review.
- **Unbalanced-Attempt Report** (diagnostic, Finance Manager/CFO only) — logged attempts that failed the
  balance check at submit/post time, useful for training and catching recurring data-entry mistakes.

# Audit Trail

Every mutation to `journal_entries`/`journal_lines`/`journal_entry_approvals` writes both:

1. A row in the foundation `audit_logs` table: `auditable_type = 'journal_entry'`, `auditable_id`,
   `action` (created/updated/submitted/approved/rejected/posted/reversed/archived/reopened), `user_id`,
   `old_values` (JSONB), `new_values` (JSONB), `reason` (nullable free text, mandatory for
   reject/reverse/reopen), `ip_address`, `user_agent`/`device`, `created_at`.
2. A row in `journal_entry_history` with the full JSONB snapshot at that version, for fast whole-entry
   reconstruction without replaying many `audit_logs` diffs.

Both are append-only and never modified or hard-deleted, for the lifetime of the company's QAYD tenancy.
`GET /journal-entries/{id}/history` merges both sources into a single chronological timeline for the UI
and for any auditor export. Because posted entries are immutable at the database trigger level (see
"Locking Rules"), the audit trail for a posted entry after posting consists solely of `reversed`/`archived`
transitions — there is structurally no way to generate an audit-log entry for an unauthorized edit to a
posted line, because no such edit can occur.

# Performance

- **Table partitioning.** `journal_entries` and `journal_lines` are range-partitioned by `fiscal_year_id`
  once a company's entry volume crosses a configured threshold (typically >2M lines/year), keeping indexes
  small and Trial Balance queries fast even for companies with years of history.
- **`ledger_entries` projection.** The GL read model is written synchronously in the same transaction as
  posting (see "Posting Engine"), so reporting queries never join across `journal_entries`/`journal_lines`
  for standard Trial Balance/GL views — they read the flat, denormalized projection instead, which is
  indexed on `(company_id, account_id, fiscal_period_id)`.
- **Redis caching** of frequently-read, rarely-changing lookups used during validation (active account
  list, open fiscal period per company, approval-threshold settings) with a short TTL and explicit
  invalidation on the relevant write.
- **Cursor pagination** on `GET /journal-entries` for companies with large registers, avoiding the
  performance cliff of `OFFSET`-based pagination on deep pages.
- **Batch posting** for bulk-imported entries processes in chunks (default 100 per DB transaction) to keep
  individual transactions short and avoid long-held row locks on `journal_number_sequences`.
- **Full-text search** (`reference`, `memo`, line `description`) uses PostgreSQL `pg_trgm`/`tsvector` GIN
  indexes rather than `LIKE '%...%'` scans.

# Background Jobs

| Job | Schedule | Responsibility |
|---|---|---|
| `GenerateRecurringJournalEntries` | Daily, per company timezone | Materializes due `journal_entry_templates` into drafts (or auto-posts if `auto_post = true` and whitelisted) |
| `RunAssetDepreciation` | Monthly (or per company's depreciation frequency) | Posts the Depreciation automatic entry for every eligible asset |
| `RevalueForeignCurrencyBalances` | At period-end close initiation | Posts the Currency Revaluation entries for all open foreign-currency monetary balances |
| `PostAccruedLoanInterest` | Monthly / per loan schedule | Posts accrued, unpaid interest on outstanding loans |
| `RunYearClosingEntry` | On fiscal-year close action | Generates and posts the Year Closing entry once all periods are `locked` |
| `AIJournalDuplicateScan` | Near-real-time, queued on every draft creation/submit | Calls the FastAPI Duplicate Detection agent and attaches warnings |
| `AIFraudRiskScan` | Near-real-time, queued on every submit | Calls the FastAPI Fraud Detection agent and escalates approval routing when flagged |
| `RefreshLedgerProjectionConsistencyCheck` | Nightly | Reconciles `ledger_entries` against `journal_lines` and alerts Finance Manager/CFO on any drift (defense-in-depth against a projection bug) |
| `ApprovalOverdueReminder` | Hourly | Scans `journal_entry_approvals` for stale `pending` rows and fires reminders |
| `ArchiveOldPostedEntries` | Monthly | Auto-archives (visibility only) entries older than a company-configured retention window with no open dependency |

All jobs run on Redis-backed Laravel queues, are idempotent (safe to re-run on retry), and emit their own
`audit_logs` entries when they cause a financial posting (depreciation, revaluation, interest accrual,
year closing) so a system-originated entry is exactly as traceable as a human-originated one.

# Security

- **Tenant isolation.** Every query is scoped by `company_id` at the Repository layer via a global Eloquent
  scope bound to the authenticated request's `X-Company-Id`; there is no code path that can return
  `journal_entries`/`journal_lines` across companies, and this is covered by a dedicated cross-tenant-leak
  test suite.
- **Row-level authorization.** Beyond RBAC permission keys, Laravel Policies enforce instance-level rules
  (e.g. "may edit only own drafts unless Finance Manager/CFO," "may not approve own submission").
- **Encryption.** PostgreSQL connections use TLS in transit; `memo`/`description` free-text fields are not
  separately field-encrypted (they are not classified as secrets), but the underlying database volume is
  encrypted at rest per the platform's infrastructure baseline.
- **Attachment access control.** Journal-entry attachments in Cloudflare R2 are never publicly readable;
  access is via short-lived signed URLs issued only after the requesting user's `accounting.journal.read`
  (and company scope) is verified.
- **Rate limiting.** Write endpoints (`create`, `submit`, `post`, `reverse`, `bulk-import`) are rate-limited
  per user and per company to prevent runaway automation (including a misbehaving AI integration) from
  flooding the ledger.
- **AI boundary enforcement.** The FastAPI layer authenticates to the Laravel API with a scoped service
  token tied to a specific `ai_conversations` context and the *acting human user's* permission set — it
  can never hold broader permissions than the user on whose behalf it is drafting, and it cannot call
  `approve`/`post`/`reverse` endpoints at all (those routes are excluded from the AI service token's scope
  entirely, not merely permission-checked).
- **Immutable-by-construction posted data.** As detailed in "Locking Rules," posted-line immutability is
  enforced redundantly at the Service layer and at the database trigger layer, so a SQL-level compromise or
  an application bug cannot silently alter posted financial history without also violating a database
  constraint that raises and rolls back.
- **Segregation of duties** as described in "Approval Workflow" is itself a security control against
  insider fraud, not merely a workflow nicety.

# Edge Cases

| Edge case | Resolution |
|---|---|
| Multi-currency rounding leaves a sub-cent difference at posting | If the base-currency difference is below the company's configured rounding tolerance (default `0.0100`), the Posting Engine requires (does not auto-generate) an explicit plug line against a `Rounding Gain/Loss` account before it will post; above tolerance, posting is hard-rejected |
| Two users edit the same draft concurrently | Optimistic concurrency via `version`; the second writer receives `409 Conflict` and must reload |
| An entry is dated into a period that becomes `locked` while it sits in `draft`/`pending_approval` | Posting fails with a period-lock error at post time (re-checked, not assumed from submit time); user must re-date into an open period or request period reopening |
| A partial reversal is needed (only some lines of a multi-line entry were wrong) | Not supported directly — QAYD reverses the *entire* entry and posts a fresh, fully correct replacement entry, preserving the "correction via new balanced entry" invariant rather than allowing partial, harder-to-audit patches |
| An account referenced by a posted entry is later deactivated | The posted entry and its historical reporting are entirely unaffected (soft-deactivation never touches historical `journal_lines`); only *new* postings to that account are blocked |
| Approver is the same person as the creator, and the company has only one qualifying user | Documented single-user exception, requires the account to be flagged accordingly by an Owner, and every self-approval under this exception is tagged distinctly in `audit_logs` for heightened review |
| An entry with zero lines or exactly one line is submitted | Rejected at the FormRequest layer before it ever reaches the Service — a single-line entry can never balance except trivially at zero, which the system also rejects as a no-op entry |
| AI proposes a duplicate of an entry already posted | Duplicate Detection flags it before submit; if a human submits anyway, the entry is not auto-blocked (false positives happen — e.g. two legitimately identical recurring accruals in different periods) but the flag and the human's override decision are both retained in `audit_logs` |
| A recurring template's formula-based line cannot resolve (e.g. references a deleted account) | The `GenerateRecurringJournalEntries` job skips that run, marks the template `status = 'paused'`, and notifies the template's owner rather than generating a broken draft |
| Exchange rate unavailable for a required currency/date combination | Posting is blocked with a specific `FX_RATE_UNAVAILABLE` error; the entry can be saved as `draft` indefinitely but cannot be submitted or posted until a rate is available or manually overridden by a Finance Manager with an audited manual-rate justification |
| Reversing an entry that was itself already partially offset by a manual adjusting entry | Permitted — QAYD does not attempt to detect "logical" offsetting across unrelated entries; only a system-generated `reversal_entry_id` link counts as "already reversed" for lifecycle purposes, so this scenario simply produces one more fully independent, fully traceable entry |
| Bulk import file contains rows in a currency the company hasn't configured | Those rows fail individually with `CURRENCY_NOT_CONFIGURED`; the rest of the batch still imports (partial success is reported explicitly, never a silent all-or-nothing rollback of an otherwise-valid batch) |
| A company reopens a locked period, posts a correction, then re-locks it | Fully supported and fully audited; the reopen event, the new posting, and the re-lock are three separate, separately timestamped `audit_logs` rows, and the External Auditor role (if configured) is notified at the reopen step regardless of what happens afterward |
| An `ai_generated` entry is approved but the underlying `ai_conversations` thread is later deleted for retention-policy reasons | The journal entry and its `ai_confidence`/`ai_conversation_id` reference remain intact; `ai_conversation_id` becomes a "dangling but harmless" historical pointer — the financial record never depends on the conversational thread surviving |
| Two automatic-entry Listeners fire for the same source event due to an at-least-once queue redelivery | The idempotency key check (`source_module + source_type + source_id + event_name`) makes the second Listener invocation a no-op; no duplicate entry is created |

# Future Improvements

- **Intercompany elimination entries** for group/holding-company structures, once multi-company
  consolidation is prioritized on the platform roadmap — today, cross-company allocations are explicitly
  out of scope and only single-company cost-center allocations are supported.
- **Partial-line reversal / split correction** as a more granular alternative to whole-entry reversal, for
  companies that specifically request line-level correction workflows, with a stricter audit-trail design
  to preserve traceability.
- **Natural-language journal entry creation** via a conversational QAYD AI interface ("accrue July rent for
  the HQ branch") that drafts a fully-formed entry from a plain-language instruction, building on the
  existing `ai_conversations`/`ai_messages` foundation and the General Accountant agent's existing
  entry-drafting capability.
- **XBRL/regulatory export** of posted journal data for jurisdictions that require structured regulatory
  filing formats beyond CSV/XLSX/PDF.
- **Real-time collaborative multi-user editing** of a single draft entry (currently single-editor with
  optimistic locking), for larger finance teams working the same complex adjusting entry together.
- **Machine-learning anomaly scoring v2**, extending today's rule-informed Fraud Detection agent with a
  learned model trained on each company's own historical posting patterns (opt-in, privacy-preserving,
  never trained across company boundaries per the platform's tenant-isolation guarantee).
- **Blockchain-anchored audit-trail hashing** for companies in regulated industries wanting an additional,
  externally-verifiable tamper-evidence layer on top of the existing immutable `audit_logs`/
  `journal_entry_history` design.
